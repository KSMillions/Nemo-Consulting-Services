Preserving Legacy Skills

Building South Africa's
Future Foremen

The construction industry is losing critical site-supervision intelligence faster than it can be replaced.

Nemo's Skill Transfer Programme bridges this gap. We take young graduates from TVET colleges and universities, pairing them directly with veteran master foremen on live building projects. They learn the trade from the foundation up, ensuring legacy site supervision standards are carried into the future.

Step 01

Graduate Onboarding

We recruit top-performing candidates directly from technical colleges and university built-environment programs, evaluating their physical aptitude and leadership drive.

Step 02

Hands-on Mentorship

Trainees are embedded under a senior Nemo general foreman on active commercial sites, learning construction sequencing, labor coordination, and quality standards.

Step 03

4IR Tech Integration

We train supervisors to use mechanical-aided tech and digital site viewers, bringing structural layout precision on par with global innovation standards.

Step 04

OHS Safety Certification

Trainees undergo strict occupational health and safety competence reviews, certifying them to coordinate sites safely before independent mobilization.

Leadership Profile

Sipho Nemo

Sipho Nemo founded Nemo Consulting Services after accumulating over 15 years of hands-on structural and building experience. He did not build the business from an office, but from the concrete slabs and steel structures of major works.

Having coordinated structural phases on high-impact projects such as the Gautrain Rosebank Tunnel, Peter Mokaba Stadium, and the Houghton Hotel, Sipho identified a recurring site vulnerability: the disconnect between complex consultant designs and on-site trade execution.
